# Pool Covers & Reels: Cutting Evaporation, Heating and Chemical Costs

In the Gulf, an open pool is an open expense. Long hours of intense sun, dry desert air and warm winds combine to pull water, heat and chemicals out of your pool faster than almost anywhere else in the world. The single most effective fix is also one of the most overlooked: a properly specified cover paired with a reliable reel. Done well, it is the closest thing to a free upgrade your pool will ever get, quietly reducing running costs every day it sits in place.

## Why Evaporation Hits UAE Pools Hardest

Evaporation is driven by surface area, heat, low humidity and wind, and a typical Dubai or Abu Dhabi summer delivers all four in abundance. An uncovered residential pool can lose several centimetres of water across a week, which means constant top-ups, higher water bills and a steady dilution of your carefully balanced chemistry.

Every litre that evaporates also carries away heat, so your heating or chilling system works harder to hold a comfortable temperature. A surface cover acts as a physical barrier between the water and the dry air above it. Independent testing consistently shows that a fitted cover can cut evaporation by around 90 to 95 per cent, which translates directly into less make-up water, fewer chemical corrections and a more stable pool overall.

## How Covers Save on Heating and Chemicals

Most of a pool's heat loss happens at the surface, so trapping it there is where the real savings sit. A solar or thermal cover slows night-time heat loss and, in the case of bubble-style covers, captures daytime warmth. In our climate that often means you can run a heat pump for fewer hours in the cooler months, or ease the load on a chiller through peak summer, with a meaningful drop in energy use.

Chemical savings are just as compelling. Sunlight rapidly breaks down chlorine, so an exposed pool burns through sanitiser to no productive end. A cover shades the water, protects your chlorine residual and reduces the stabiliser and shock you would otherwise need. It also keeps out the fine Gulf dust, leaves and debris that drive up filtration and cleaning effort. Lower evaporation means less concentrated, more predictable water chemistry, so you spend less on chemicals and less time managing them.

## Choosing the Right Cover and Reel

The best cover is the one that actually gets used, which is why the reel matters as much as the cover itself. Thermal bubble covers offer strong value for heat and evaporation control. Slatted automatic covers add safety and a premium finish, while solid safety covers suit pools where child or pet protection is the priority. For spas, a fitted insulated lid is essential; for ponds and ornamental fountains, evaporation management protects both water level and pump health.

A quality reel turns covering into a ten-second, one-person job rather than a chore that gets skipped. Specify reels built for UAE conditions: UV-stable materials, corrosion-resistant fittings and a span correctly sized to your pool. For larger or commercial pools, motorised and recessed systems remove the manual effort entirely and keep poolside lines clean.

## Practical Tips for Gulf Conditions

Cover the pool whenever it is not in use, especially overnight and during the hottest part of the day. Choose UV-rated materials, as cheaper covers degrade quickly under Gulf sun and shed into the water. Keep the cover and reel rinsed and store the cover off the deck when removed for long periods. Finally, re-check your chemistry after sealing a pool that was previously open, as a covered pool holds chlorine far longer and is easily over-dosed.
